The Life of Rue McClanahan

Eddie-Rue McClanahan, born on February 21, 1934, was a renowned American TV and stage actress, best known for her role in the sitcom “The Golden Girls”.

Her journey to a $5 million net worth began in early 2016, largely fueled by her extensive acting career.

Rue’s Roots and Rise

Originally from Healdton, Oklahoma, Rue McClanahan was born to Dreda Rheua-Nell and William Edwin McClanahan. She opted for an acting career, forgoing a dance scholarship after high school, and pursued studies in German and Theater at the University of Tulsa.

Starting with small roles in productions like “Inherit the Wind” at Erie Playhouse, she transitioned to Broadway. Norman Lear, a TV exec, recognized her talent, providing a break in shows like “All in the Family” and “Maude”, eventually landing her iconic role in “The Golden Girls”.

Following the sitcom’s success, Rue featured in various TV shows and movies, such as “Out to Sea” and “Law & Order”. A prolific career spanning 60+ years included over 30 films, 50+ TV productions, and a Broadway stint with “Wicked”.

Outside work, Rue was an advocate for animal rights, supporting organizations like PETA. She bravely battled breast cancer in 1997 and underwent a successful bypass surgery in 2009 before passing away in 2010 in New York at 76 due to a brain hemorrhage.
