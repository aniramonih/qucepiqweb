Early Life

Paul Labile Pogba was born on 15th March 1993 in Lagny-Sur-Marne, France. His parents are Guinean, and he was brought up as a Muslim. He has two elder twin brothers, and both of them are also professional footballers. His eldest brother, Florentin is at present in the French club, Saint-Étienne. The other twin, Mathias plays for Sparta Rotterdam, which is a Dutch club and is also in the Guinean National Team. While growing up, Paul’s favorite club was Arsenal F.C.

He began his football training from a very young age and officially started his career when he was merely six. He played for US Roissy-en-Brie, a club few miles south to where he lived. He has changed quite a few clubs over the course of his career, but is now back with Manchester United and is enumerated amongst the world’s most expensive players in history.

Career

Paul Pogba joined the US Torcy, as the captain of their Under-13 team after he quit his initial club, where he had played for seven seasons. However, he was associated with US Torcy for a single season only and soon shifted to a professional club named Le Havre. He was promoted to serve as the captain of the Club’s Under-16 team in the second season.

His international career also kick-started with the National Youth Football team of France when he has announced the captain of the under-16 team. In 2009, Paul decided to join the Youth Academy of Manchester United. He was transferred there by the 7th of October 2009. He started playing in the club’s under-18 team.

In February 2011, he was amongst the four players who were promoted to the first-team squad. Later, it was confirmed by the club that Paul had left it on 3rd July 2012. He then went on to sign a four-year contract with Juventus. In 2013, he was transferred to the French Senior team for the 2014 World Cup Qualifier. He made a return to Manchester United after signing a five-year contract with the club on the 8th of August 2016.

Although the football association barred him from playing the Opening Premier League since he managed to get two yellow cards in the preceding season, he was allowed to play in the Premier League from 19th August 2016. Recently in the 2017-18 UEFA Champions’ League group stage, Paul suffered a ligament injury and had to miss eight league matches.
