Marion Jolles’ bio

Marion is a well-known French journalist and television presenter best recognized for her work on the show Automoto. She was born on December 8, 1981, in Meaux, France. She has completed her journalism education with DESS (Diplome Detudes Superieures Specialiesees). She also earned her master’s degree in English in France.

She also spent six months studying in Canada. She has a lengthy history in journalism and is a true professional in her area. She is also well known for being the wife of French racing driver Romain Grosjean. Marion started her career in 2005 by working for the European sports channel Eurosport. Later, she began working as a presenter on a brief show called La Bonne Conduite, which focused on road safety and was broadcast on the TF1 channel.

The same year, she was selected as the host of a popular show called Automoto, which helped her achieve renown. She later began co-hosting the show F1 a la une with Denis Brogniart. It was only in effect until 2012. On the TF1 channel, she also hosted a show called Confessions Intimes.
