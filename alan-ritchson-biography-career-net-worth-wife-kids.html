Alan Ritchson biography, career, net worth, wife, kids

Who is Alan Ritchson?

Alan Michael Ritchson is an actor, model, singer, and songwriter from the United States. Between the fifth and tenth seasons of The CW superhero series Smallville, when he guest starred as Aquaman / Arthur Curry, he made his acting debut.

Alan Ritchson bio

Alan Ritchson was born in Grand Forks, North Dakota on November 28, 1982, as the second of three boys to high school teacher Vickie and retired US Air Force Chief Master Sergeant David. Ritchson lived in Rantoul, Illinois as a child before settling in Niceville, Florida.

Alan Ritchson education

He attended Niceville High School there, where he eventually graduated in 2001. After attending Okaloosa Walton Community College, which is now known as Northwest Florida State College, Ritchson went on to get an associate of arts degree.

Alan Ritchson career

Alan Ritchson first became well-known for his portrayal of Arthur Curry in the superhero television series “Smallville.” Later, he appeared as a lead in television shows like “Reacher,” “Blue Mountain State,” “Blood Drive,” and “Titans.” Ritchson has been in movies like “The Hunger Games: Catching Fire” and “Teenage Mutant Ninja Turtles” on the big screen. In 2021, he made his directorial debut with “Dark Web: Cicada 3301.”

Ritchson’s modeling career began with appearances in the Abercrombie & Fitch catalog. When he participated in the reality television singing competition “American Idol” in 2004 and placed among the top 87 competitors, he attracted more notice.

In 2005, Ritchson made his acting debut and landed his breakout role. On the superhero television series “Smallville,” he started appearing as Arthur Curry, also known as Aquaman, in that year. In the show’s fifth season and again in its tenth and final season in 2010, Ritchson made two appearances.

He made appearances in the television movies “Though None Go with Me” and “Midnight Bayou” during this time. In the comedy “Blue Mountain State,” Ritchson played Kevin Devlin “Thad” Castle, the captain of the collegiate football team, from 2010 to 2011.

Ritchson continued to feature in different television programs, such as “CSI: Miami,” “90210,” “Hawaii Five-0,” “New Girl,” “Workaholics,” and “Black Mirror.”

Additionally, he frequently appeared on the celebrity competition show “I Can Do That,” which NBC broadcast for one season in 2015.

Ritchson played LAPD officer Arthur Bailey in the ephemeral Syfy series “Blood Drive” in 2017. Christina Ochoa, Marama Corlett, Colin Cunningham, and Thomas Dominique served as his co-stars. Then, in 2018, Ritchson made a comeback to the superhero genre by taking on the character of Hank Hall in the DC Universe series “Titans.”

Although he had only been contracted for two episodes at first, he was given a significant role for the second and third seasons. On “Supergirl” and “Legends of Tomorrow” from the DC Universe, Ritchson returned in the role of Hank Hall.

He started playing the title character on the Amazon Prime Video series “Reacher,” which is based on the “Jack Reacher” novel series, in 2022. This was his next major television role.

Ritchson made his feature film debut in the 2006 horror movie “The Butcher,” in which he played one of the adolescents who, following a car accident, are haunted by a family of psychopaths.

The drama “Steam,” starring Ruby Dee, Kate Siegel, and Ally Sheedy, was the next film in which he had an appearance. A few years later, Ritchson appeared in the teen sex comedy “Fired Up!” as a member of the big ensemble cast. He then got parts in some important Hollywood movies.

In the science fiction prequel “The Hunger Games: Catching Fire,” Ritchson portrayed the character Gloss in 2013. He played Raphael in the Teenage Mutant Ninja Turtles reboot the following year, and he would play the character again in the 2016 follow-up, “Teenage Mutant Ninja Turtles: Out of the Shadows.”

Between the two movies, Ritchson starred in “The Wedding Ringer,” a romantic comedy, and “Lazer Team,” an action comedy with a science fiction theme. In “Blue Mountain State: The Rise of Thadland,” he played Thad Castle once more in the hit television series “Blue Mountain State.”

In the horror comedy “Office Uprising” from 2018, Ritchson costarred with Zachary Levi, Brenton Thwaites, Jane Levy, Karan Soni, and others. Then, in 2019 he appeared in two movies: “Above the Shadows,” a mystical romance starring Olivia Thirlby and Jim Gaffigan, and “The Turkey Bowl,” a sports comedy starring Matt Jones and Ryan Hansen.

In the supernatural horror movie “Ghosts of War” the following year, Ritchson reconnected with his “Office Uprising” co-star Brenton Thwaites. With the comedy-thriller “Dark Web: Cicada 3301,” which he also co-wrote and co-produced, Ritchson made his directorial debut in 2021. In addition, he co-stars in the movie with Conor Leslie, Ron Funches, and Jack Kesy.
