Central Cee Speaks About Split with Madeline Argy in New Song

Central Cee dropped his latest track on Monday — and the lyrics appear to be about his break-up with Madeline Argy.

Is Central Cee’s New Song About Split With Madeline Argy?

  • In September, it was reported that Central Cee and TikTok star Madeline Argy had split up after two years of dating.
  • On the Call Her Daddy podcast, Madeline finally confirmed her breakup with Central Cee and discussed their “toxic” relationship. She says she dumped the rapper — which was “hard” because “we were a little bit obsessed with each other.”
  • Central Cee has never publicly discussed the end of his relationship with Pretty Lonesome podcaster Madeline. However, on Monday, the rapper dropped his new track on Instagram and the lyrics seem to refer to the heartbreaking split with the TikTok star.
  • Thousands of fans immediately left comments about his ex Madeline on the post. A fan writes: “Madz hurt him real bad huh” “We know you talking about Madeline,” another viewer comments.

    •  

What does Central Cee Allegedly Say About His Break-up With Madeline?

  • Central Cee’s new track — which was titled “there’s truth to the lies” — samples the break-up song So Sick by Ne-Yo.” He raps about heartbreak saying: “Without your love, feeling suicidal but I keep my head up.”

    •  


     
  • Central Cee also raps: “She says I’m a slut, don’t give a f*ck. I agree with her.” Fans speculated that the lyrics appear to refer to the viral moment when Madeline posted a photo of her eating potato chips and looking at an Instagram post of Central Cee holding cash at a strip club.
  • Central Cee also seems to allude to the fact that the woman is in a different country to him while he tours in the U.S.: “Travel round the world and you’re the only one that’s on my mind.” Madeline lives in the U.K.

    •  
     

  • Central Cee also seems to cite Madeline’s explosive interview on Call Her Daddy and her devastating conclusion on their relationship: “Baby if I knew you would act like that, I would save you the stress. I don’t want to see you mad like that. You make me regret.”
  • In the song, the rapper pleads with an ex-girlfriend to get back together and how she has ended all communication with him: “Let’s restart to the day you that we met. I done you wrong and now you’re cold.” Madeline says she has stopped all communication with Central Cee and declined to meet up when the rapper contacted her for the last time.
  • Central Cee also describes calling his ex up but she has blocked him: “I hit you up, you block the number.” The song then features a British voicemail with seemingly appears to relate to Madeline.
  • At the end of the track, Central Cee fantasizes about getting back together with his ex. He raps: “Wherever I’m at, I’ve got time for you babe. I know it’s hard, we’re on different time zones and shit. But it helps that we’re both young in the public eye and we live similar lifestyles.” Fans took this to be another reference to Madeline and her own celebrity career.
