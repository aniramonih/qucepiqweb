Is Mariah Carey Related To Whitney Houston?

Is Mariah Carey related to Whitney Houston? Learn the truth about the two legendary singers’ relationship.

The American singer-songwriter, Mariah, is well-known for her five-octave vocal range, signature use of the whistle register, and melismatic singing style.

Referred to as the “Songbird Supreme,” she first gained fame in 1990 following her self-titled debut album Mariah Carey.

Her biggest hits include All I Want For Christmas Is You; We Belong Together, Always Be My Baby, Fantasy, Obsessed, Hero, and Honey.

Additionally, Mariah is an Actress with roles in Precious, Mariah’s World, The Butler, A Christmas Melody, and The Lego Batman Movie, among others.

Is Mariah Carey Related To Whitney Houston?

Mariah Carey and Whitney Houston are two highly talented and admired vocalists. But, the music legends are not related.

The two singers were acquaintances and friends from the music world and dueted on multiple occasions. They collaborated on a 1998 song called When You Believe.

The song received the Academy Award for Best Original Song at the 71st ceremony in 1999.

Before their performance at the Oscars, Mariah and Whitney sang it on The Oprah Winfrey Show in November 1998.

There were rumors that the two had a big feud, but Mariah denied the speculations and said the “feud” rumors were only the media looking for Drama. 

She said that she and Houston did not know each other previously but became thick as thieves after they worked together in 1998. 

Following Houston’s death, Mariah said on Good Morning America in 2012, “I loved her. We all loved her. May she rest in peace… her legend will go on forever.”

The Songbird Supreme Singer Family And Ethnicity

Mariah Carey was born in Huntington, New York, United States, to her parents, Patricia (née Hickey) and Alfred Roy Carey.

Her mother, Patricia, is a former opera singer and vocal coach, while her Father, Alfred Roy Carey, is an aeronautical engineer.

Mariah comes from a mixed ethnic background as her mom is of Irish descent and her dad of African-American and black Venezuelan-American roots.

Born on March 27, 1969, Mariah is currently 54, and her star sign is Aries.

She is the youngest of three children, including her sister Alison and brother Morgan.

Her parents set her first name from the 1952 Broadway musical song ‘They Call the Wind Maria.’

Patricia’s family abandoned her for marrying a black man. Following her parents’ divorce, Mariah had minimal contact with her Father, while her mother held several jobs to support the family. 

From her early years in school, Mariah excelled in the arts, like music and literature. She attended Harborfields High School when she began writing poetry and lyrics.

She also started vocal training under her mother’s tutelage. Despite her background in classical opera, Patricia was always supportive and never forced her daughter to pursue a career in it.

Mariah Carey Religion Is Episcopalianism

Mariah Carey is a Christian, more specifically, Episcopalian.

Episcopalians, commonly known as Anglicans, derive from rituals and practices of the Church of England after the English Reformation.

They are Christians who ground their belief and faith in the teachings of the Bible.

Faith is central to Mariah, as is evidenced by her gospel songs on her several albums and public praise of God for her career longevity.

Her religion was also apparent when she married her first husband, Tommy Mottola, at St. Thomas Episcopal Church in New York in 1993.

The All I Want For Christmas Is You singer strongly believes in God. She is vocal about her profound gratitude to God for helping her through dark periods.

Mariah has also attended church services and performed in the church over the years.

Her former husband, Nick Cannon, has mentioned that they bonded over their mutual spiritual beliefs. 

Nick added that Mariah is conservative and “deep into her Bible.”
