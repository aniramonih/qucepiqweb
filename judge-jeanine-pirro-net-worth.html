Jeanine Ferris Pirro: A Look at Her Net Worth

Wondering about Jeanine Ferris Pirro’s net worth? It’s estimated at $5 Million


A Glance at Jeanine Ferris Pirro’s Life

Born on June 2, 1951 in Elmira, New York, Jeanine Pirro comes from a mixed Lebanese, Antiochian, Greek Orthodox and Catholic descent. She is known for her work as a judge, prosecutor, legal analyst, and TV personality. Jeanine has been the Chairwoman of the New York State Commission on Domestic Violence Fatalities since 1997, and she has been featuring in the reality TV legal show “Justice with Judge Jeanine” on Fox News Channel since 2011.

Her net worth amounts to $5 million, earned over her law career that started in the 1970s.

Judge Jeanine Pirro’s Early Life and Education

Jeanine was raised in Elmira by Esther and Nasser Pirro. She attended Notre Dame High School and later obtained a Bachelor’s degree from the University of Buffalo. She also holds a Juris Doctor degree from Albany Law School, earned in 1975.

Her legal career started as a Westchester County Assistant District Attorney in 1975, during which she created one of the first Domestic Violence Units in the US. Jeanine made history by being elected as a Westchester County judge in 1990, and later became the first female District Attorney of the County in 1993. Even after stepping down from re-election in 2005, her contributions continued.

Aside from law, Jeanine delved into politics, serving on the New York State Commission on Domestic Violence Fatalities starting in 1997. In 2006, she ran for the position of Senator of New York but was defeated by Hillary Clinton.

On the small screen, Jeanine has been a guest and contributor on various TV shows such as “The Morning Show with Mike and Juliet”, “Good Day New York”, “Today”, and “Fox News”, as well as hosting her own shows like “Justice with Judge Jeanine”. Her involvement in television significantly impacted her net worth.

In addition to her TV career, Jeanine has authored several books like “To Punish and Protect” (2003), describing life in the criminal justice system, and co-authored the novel “Sly Fox” (2012) based on her own experiences.

Despite personal challenges, including her husband’s legal issues, Jeanine continues to thrive in her TV career.
