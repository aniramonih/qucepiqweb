James Marsden Bio (Wiki)

Who is James Marsden?

James Marsden is an American actor, singer, and former model who has appeared in various Tv shows and films such as The Notebook, Superman Returns, and The Alibi.

Likewise, he is known as Scott Summers / Cyclops in the superhero film series, X-Men.

Early Life, Birth Facts, Parents

James Marsden was born on September 18, 1973, in Stillwater, Oklahoma, USA.

As of 2022, James Marsden will be celebrating his 49th birthday. His full name is James Paul Marsden.

Likewise, he is the son of James Luther Marsden, a food safety advisor, and Kathleen, a nutritionist.

When he was nine years old, his parents got divorced. Similarly, he has two brothers named Robbie Marsden and Jeff Marsden.

His sisters are Elizabeth Marsden and Jennifer Marsden. Similarly, he was interested in acting at a very young age.

James Marsden is of American Nationality and his ethnicity is unknown. Moreover, his birth sign is Virgo.

Education, Academic Qualifications

James attended Hefner Middle School and Putnam City North High School both situated in Oklahoma City.

Later, James Marsden pursued his education in broadcast journalism at Oklahoma State University in Oklahoma.

Moreover, he was also a part of the Delta Tau Delta fraternity.

Career, Professional Life

Having started his acting career in 1993 with a minor role in the action drama movie In the Line of Duty: Ambush in Waco.

Later James Marsden appeared in the Tv series ‘The Nanny’ as Eddie in the same year followed by another Tv series ‘Boogies Diner’ in 1994.

After that, there is no looking backward and he has gained more than 75 acting credits to date.

Some of his most notable appearances on television include The Notebook in 2004, and the superhero film Superman Returns in 2006, and this was followed by another superhero film X-Men: The Last Stand in 2006.

James Marsden has also appeared on Sex Drive (2008), The Box (2009), Straw Dogs (2011), Enchanted (2007) and Hop (2011), 27 Dresses (2007), The Best of Me (2014), and Sonic the Hedgehog (2020) among others.

Furthermore, his latest movies are Mrs. America, The Stand, and Dead to Me.
