FAQs About Jay London

Who is Jay London?

Jay London is an American stand-up comedian known for his one-liner jokes and appearances on NBC’s Last Comic Standing during its second and third seasons in 2004. Despite not winning, his humble personality and clean comedy made him a fan favorite.

When was he born?

He was born on September 12, 1966.

Where did he grow up?

He was born in The Bronx but grew up in Flushing, Queens. Later, he moved to Los Angeles in his adult years.

What did he do before becoming a comedian?

Before entering comedy, he worked in various jobs, including being a boxer and a taxicab driver.

What is his comedy style?

He is recognized for his long curly hair, bib overalls, and self-deprecating humor.

He often uses the signature line, You might recognize him, he’s the fourth guy from the left on the evolutionary chart, which references his slouching posture and unkempt appearance. His comedy includes quips and his trademark line, It’s almost over.

Has he appeared on any television shows?

Beyond Last Comic Standing, he has appeared on The Tonight Show with Jay Leno, the CBS sitcom The King of Queens, the TBS reality show Minding the Store, and Bravo’s All-Star Reality Reunion.

He also launched The Jay London Show on his website in 2007.

What is The Jay London Show?

The Jay London Show is a television pilot that debuted on his official website in January 2007, where he co-stars with his real-life sister, Melanie.

Has he worked with other comedians?

After Last Comic Standing, he joined Gary Gulman and Alonzo Bodden for the I’m Still Standing comedy tour and opened for Louis C.K. during his 2015 stand-up special Live At The Comedy Store.
