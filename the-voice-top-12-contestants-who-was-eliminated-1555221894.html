The Voice Top 12 Contestants, Who was Eliminated?

Explore The Voice Season 24's thrilling journey with a revamped coaching lineup, featuring Niall Horan, John Legend, Gwen Stefani, and newcomer Reba McEntire. Find the Top 12 contestants and find out who faced elimination in the recent intense battles.

The Voice (American Season 24)

The Voice's twenty-fourth season is on September 25, 2023, on NBC, with Carson Daly reprising his role as the host. The coaching panel underwent significant changes, featuring Niall Horan in his second consecutive season, alongside the return of John Legend and Gwen Stefani after a one-season hiatus. Additionally, Reba McEntire joined the coaching lineup as a newcomer.

Notably, this season marks the first time without Blake Shelton as a coach, representing a departure from the original coaches of the show's inaugural season. The revamped coaching lineup promises a fresh and dynamic experience for both contestants and viewers in this latest installment of the beloved reality television series.

The Voice Top 12 Contestants

In the recent Top 12 Results Show of The Voice Season 24, the eliminated contestants were Azán from Team Legend, Tanner Massey from Team Gwen, and Kara Tenae from Team Gwen. The Wildcard Instant Save was claimed by Jordan Rainer from Team Reba, ensuring her continuation in the competition.

The episode featured a range of performances, including Jordan's rendition of Patty Loveless' "Blame It On Your Heart," Azán's take on Ariana Grande's "No Tears Left to Cry," and Tanner Massey's cover of Extreme's "More Than Words."

The judges provided grades and comments for each performance, offering insights into the strengths and weaknesses of the contestants. The Semi-Finals are set to take place next Monday at 8/7c on NBC, where the remaining contestants will continue to vie for the title in this competitive season of The Voice.

    The Voice Battle

    In "The Voice," the Battle rounds are a big deal. They happen after the auditions, where the judges can't see the singers, and before the knockout rounds. In the Battles, the coaches make two of their team members sing together to figure out who's better. Both singers sing the same song as a duet, showing how good they are at singing and performing on stage. After they sing, the coach has to pick one to go to the next round, and the other might have to leave the competition.

    What's cool about the Battle rounds is that if a coach doesn't pick a singer, other coaches can say they want that singer for their team. It's like a surprise second chance for the singer to work with a different coach.

    The Battle rounds aren't just about singing; they also test the coaches to make smart choices for their teams. These rounds help decide who will go to the next round called the knockouts, where singers face new challenges and performances. In the end, "The Voice" Battle rounds are a really important and exciting part of the show that adds a lot of drama and excitement.
