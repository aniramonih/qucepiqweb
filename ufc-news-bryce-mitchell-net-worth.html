Bryce Mitchell’s MMA career

Mitchell’s persona was built on the ability to give to the fans a working man’s fighter – that is, he is honest to the core, says it like it is, and gets the job done without much of a fuss. And his MMA career reflects just that, with his impressive record in the UFC – his record stands at 15-1. Before entering the UFC, he had bouts in V3 Fights, AXS TV Fights, and WSOF.

The UFC and its scouts have a special knack for scouting talent at the grassroots. They were quick to get him to The Ultimate Fighter, where he, unfortunately, lost in the quarterfinals. However, he bounced back to win six straight in the UFC.

In his career, Mitchell submitted Matt Sayles with a nasty maneuver. He has notable wins over Charles Rosa, Andre Fili, and Edson Barboza, where he wrestled away to a decision victory. However, surging contender Ilia Topuria countered Mitchell’s wrestling at UFC 282 and handed Thug Nasty his first professional loss in his career. Mitchell will aim to bounce back in his next fight and get back to his winning ways.

Bryce Mitchell’s girlfriend

Bryce Mitchell’s gym and coach

Being an Arkansas native, it only makes sense for Mitchell to train out of the state where he enjoys his farming responsibilities. Mitchell trains out of Barata MMA under the guidance of coach Rudy McGlothlin.

Being a humble fighter at heart, Mitchell has chosen to surround himself with the right team to keep him grounded, with its base as close to him as possible, which is integral to his functioning at the top. He puts in the work as suggested by his coaching team and always remains cordial with them.

A broken hand, but not as gnarly as the aforementioned story. Injury troubles have kept Mitchell out of the octagon regularly. Hopefully, his health will improve as he approaches his prime.
