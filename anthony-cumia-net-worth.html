Early Life

Anthony Cumia was born on April 26, 1961, in Queens, New York. He grew up in an Italian-American household with an older brother and younger sister. His parents separated during his early teens, and he lived with his father in Southern California before returning to New York.

Prior to his career in radio, Cumia worked in HVAC system installation. He found inspiration from influential radio personalities like Don Imus and Howard Stern during his formative years.

Career

Cumia’s career began in 1994 when he submitted an O.J. Simpson parody song to Gregg “Opie” Hughes’ radio show “Nighttime Attitude.” This collaboration eventually led to the launch of the “Opie and Anthony” show in 1995 on WAFF in Boston.

Despite facing setbacks such as being fired for a prank and station changes, “Opie and Anthony” found success when they joined XM Satellite Radio in 2004. However, the show came to an end in 2014 after Cumia was fired by SiriusXM for racially charged tweets against a black woman.

After his departure from traditional radio, Cumia started “The Anthony Cumia Show,” an audio and video podcast that has been ongoing since then. In 2018, he also published a memoir titled “Permanently Suspended: The Rise and Fall… and Rise Again of Radio’s Most Notorious Shock Jock.”

Personal Life
