Where is American actor, Demond Wilson and his model wife, Cicely Johnston

American actor and author, Demond Wilson is one of the well-known personality. He has conquered the era of the 1970s with his impressive style of acting. Some of his most loved and remembered roles are in the 1970s NBC sitcom Sanford and Son as Lamont Sanford and Oscar Madison in The New Odd Couple. His film appearance in Me and the Kid in 1993 is equally loved by the viewers.

Furthermore, he was firmly active from 1971 to 2005 in the world of film and TV series. In this long acting career, he has played numerous roles in about twenty movies and TV series combined. Interestingly, most of them are blockbuster and was viewed by a huge amount of mass.

Forty-six years long marital life with Cicely Johnston

Seventy-four year old, Demond is a passionate lover and a loyal husband. He exchanged his wedding vows with his longtime girlfriend, Cicely Johnston. Cicely is a former American model. Recalling their old days, the pair encounter one another for the very first time back in 1970. At that period of time, both were in the struggling phases of their career. 

After a couple of dates and drinks, the pair got involved in a serious relationship and decided to take their relationship one step further. On 3rd May of 1974, they announced one another as husband and wife after dating for almost three years. Despite not being in limelight the pair have still managed to take Hollywood with their impressive love life.

Net worth and Earnings

Demond has a net worth of $1.5 Million as of 2020. He has successfully gathered all of his fortunes through his impressive career as an actor and author. At the height of his career, he used to earn over $50,000 per episode. His iconic role in the Sanford and Son has led him to gather a huge amount of name and fame. 

In that particular series, he played one hundred and thirty-five episodes as a leading role from which he earned over $20 Million at the end of the season. Moreover, he has authored numerous books including Mr. Fish Takes a Wife, John Neuman Smith, and Second Banana which has a great review by the readers.
