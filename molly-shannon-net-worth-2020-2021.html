Early Life & Biography

Molly Shannon, whose full name is Molly Helen Shannon was born on 16thof September 1964 in Shaker Heights, Ohio, U.S.  She was born to an Irish American Catholic family to parents, James Francis Shannon and Mary Margaret Peg (formerly, Keating). Her father worked as a sales manager, and her mother worked as a teacher.

When she was just four years old, she was involved in a car accident that killed her mother, younger sister, and cousin. When it comes to her education, she went to St. Dominic School in Shaker Heights for grade school, and Hawken School in Gates Mills, Ohio. She then went to NYU’s Tisch School of the Arts, where she studied drama and graduated in 1987.

Career

Molly Shannon made her debut with the 1989 horror film “The Phantom of the Opera.” After that, she did play minor roles in two different shows, but her main breakthrough came in February of 1995 when she was hired as a featured player on the TV show “Saturday Night Live.”

She left SNL in 2001 but returned years later to host an episode of Saturday Night Live, which was aired on 12th May 2007. Besides, she has appeared in a special Mother’s Day episode of Saturday Night Live on 8th of May 2010 as well as October 2010 reunion special titled “Women of SNL.”

Before leaving SNL in 2001, she had starred in the movies “Never Been Kissed” (1999), “How The Grinch Stole Christmas” (2000), and “Serendipity” (2001). Her most notable film role came in the 2016 comedy-drama film “Other People,” for which she even won the 2017 Film Independent Spirit Award for Best Supporting Actress. Also, she has even performed in Broadway in the role of Marge McDougall for the musical “Promises, Promises.”  She has starred in the 2018 critically successful film “Private Life” as well.

She is into children’s book as well, having authored her first children’s book, “Tilly the Trickster,” which was released on the 1st of September 2001. She has appeared in a video for Sheryl Crow’s song “A Change (Will Do You Good).”
