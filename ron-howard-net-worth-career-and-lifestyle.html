Ron Howard Career Details 

Howard started his Career too early as a child artist. However, the actor made his directorial debut very late in his career, in 1977. He directed a low-budget comedy action film named Grand Theft Auto.

Then, Ron directed numerous TV films. Eventually, he got a big break with 1982’s Night Shift starring Michael Keaton, Henry Winkler, and Shelley Long.

The film cemented his directorial Career, and since then, he has directed many major films such as Splash, Willow, Cocoon, Backdraft, Apollo 13, Parenthood, A Beautiful Mind, The Da Vinci Code, Rush, and more.

Additionally, he directed the biggest box-office franchise, the Star Wars prequel, Solo: A Star Wars Story, in 2018.

The film was nearly completed at the time of Howards’s takeover. He only had five weeks of reshoots and three weeks of filming left.

Likewise, Howard received the Austin Film Festival’s 2009 Extraordinary Contribution to Filmmaking Award for his directorial contribution.
