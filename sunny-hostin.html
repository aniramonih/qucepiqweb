The View Co-host Sunny Hostin Shares Two Children With Her 22 Years of Husband, Emmanuel Hostin

Sunny Hostin, is a Daytime Emmy Award-winning American journalist, columnist, and TV host who is now working for ABC News as the Senior Legal Correspondent and Analyst. She is famous for co-hosting the ABC's morning talk show The View. Also, for now, she appears on Investigation Discovery's true-crime series Truth About Murder with Sunny Hostin as an executive producer.

She was born on October 20, 1968, in New York City as Asuncion Cummings Hostin. She is the daughter of African American father, William Cummings and Puerto Rican mother, Rosa Beza. Growing up in the Bronx, New York City, she attended the Dominican Academy. Further, she got her bachelor's degree from Binghamton University in English and Rhetoric.

Lawyer To Journalism Journey

Before starting a hosting career, Sunny initially made her career debut as an appellate law clerk. She became the trial attorney in the U.S. Department of Justice's Antitrust Division.

Then she became a federal prosecutor in the nation’s capital specializing in child sex crimes. She further earned a Special Achievement Award from Attorney General Janet Reno for her work prosecuting crimes against women and child sex crimes.

Hostin then started her tv career as a Court TV analyst. Afterward, she joined the Fox News program The O'Reilly Factor, where she worked on the show's "Is It Legal?" Also, from 2007 to 2011 she was a political reporter on CNN's American Morning television show. From 2011 to 2012 she was a fill-in co-anchor of ABC's World News Now.
