NHRA Pro Stock racer Angie Smith fatally injured in a horrific motorcycle accident

Madison, Illinois – Angie Smith, a renowned Pro Stock NHRA motorcycle racer hailing from King, North Carolina, faced a serious accident during the AAA Midwest Nationals on a fateful Saturday afternoon, September 30. This incident transpired at the 12th annual NHRA Midwest Nationals held at the World Wide Technology Raceway in Madison, Illinois.

Following a qualifying run at an astounding speed of 198.93 mph, Smith tragically lost control of her motorcycle. The incident, which occurred in the shutdown area after her second qualifying run, was not visible to the spectators or control tower announcers. Despite the severity of the mishap, Smith remained conscious and was in communication with the safety personnel present on-site. She was subsequently transported to a nearby hospital for further evaluation.

Prior to this unfortunate accident, Smith had held the sixth position in the Pro Stock Motorcycle standings. In the aftermath, numerous racers, including her husband Matt Smith, Jianna Evaristo, and Joey Gladstone, chose to opt out of the qualifying rounds.

The NHRA released a statement regarding the incident, stating that Angie Smith lost control of her motorcycle after crossing the finish line during the second round of Pro Stock Motorcycle qualifying at the World Wide Technology Raceway. She was examined by NHRA medical staff while still conscious before being taken to a nearby hospital for thorough examinations.

An update on Angie Smith’s health was provided on Sunday, October 1, indicating that she sustained two broken feet and severe road rash. The racer was scheduled to consult a skin graft surgeon for further treatment. The accident, occurring in the race track’s shutdown area, resulted in extensive road rash, necessitating skin grafting to aid in her recovery.

Angie Smith, a prominent figure in the NHRA Pro Stock Motorcycle division, has consistently made her mark in the field, showcasing her talent with notable achievements. The racing community stands in solidarity, sending wishes for her speedy recovery and return to the track.
