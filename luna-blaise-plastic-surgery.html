Eddie Levert’s Children — The Singer Lost Two Kids in 2006 and 2008

RIP Gerald Levert and Sean Levert!

Eddie Levert is an American musician best recognized for his work as the lead vocalist for The O’Jays. 

To date, he has seven children —  Gerald Levert, Sean Levert, Eddie Jr. Levert, Kandice Levert, Eurydice Levert, Shawn Levert, and Ryan Levert. Four of his kids, Eddie Jr., Kandice, Eurydice, and Shawn are living their life out of the spotlight.

However, three of his children, Gerald, Sean, and Ryan, followed in his footsteps into the music industry. Ryan, who recorded a song called ‘Stupid People’ with her father at 13, is currently pursuing a career in music.

Gerald and Sean, on the other hand, are no more. Levert lost both of his sons in the span of two years in 2006 and 2008, respectively. Here is all we know about it. 

Gerald Levert (July 13, 1966 – November 10, 2006)

Gerald was an American singer, composer, and producer. He was most recognized for his work with his brother, Sean, and friend, Marc Gordon, in the R&B vocal trio LeVert. 

He was also a part of LSG, a supergroup that included Keith Sweat, Johnny Gill, and himself. In his career, he released nine solo albums, six as a member of LeVert, two with his father Eddie, and two as part of LSG. 

Gerald was also credited with discovering R&B groups The Rude Boys, Men at Large, and 1 of the Girls. He was also a member of the R&B group Black Men United.

Gerald was not only a singer but also an actor. He began his acting career as Charles Young, making his debut appearance on The Jamie Foxx Show for two episodes. The first one was the episode titled ‘Just Don’t Do It’ that aired on November 5, 1998.

Gerald returned in the series finale ‘Always and Forever’ in January 2001, when Jamie (Jamie Foxx) and Fancy (Garcelle Beauvais) got married, and he sang the opening ceremony alongside other singers Gladys Knight and Marilyn McCoo. 

Gerald’s final acting performance that aired during his lifetime was on November 10, 2003, exactly three years prior to his death. He was featured in an episode of The Parkers where he played T’s father in the episode.

Gerald was found dead in his bed on November 10, 2006, at his Cleveland, Ohio residence. Initial reports indicated that he died of an apparent heart attack, but an autopsy done by the Cuyahoga County coroner’s office determined that his death was caused by a lethal mix of prescription opioids and over-the-counter medications. 

His bloodstream included narcotic pain medicines Vicodin, Percocet, and Darvocet, as well as anxiety medication Xanax and two over-the-counter antihistamines. Gerald was also diagnosed with pneumonia at the postmortem. 

The official cause of death was acute intoxication, and it was considered an accident. At the time of his death, Gerald was forty years old. 

Eddie Levert lost two of his children in the late 2000s. (Source: Twitter)

Following the announcement of Gerald’s cause of death, a family spokeswoman revealed that all of the medications discovered in his system were prescribed to the singer. He was on pain medication due to persistent shoulder discomfort and surgery in 2005 to repair a damaged Achilles tendon.

Sean Levert (September 28, 1968 – March 30, 2008)

Sean, like his brother, was an American singer, composer, and actor. He was born in Cleveland, Ohio, and created the LeVert alongside his older brother Gerald and childhood friend Marc Gordon. 

In the 1980s and early 1990s, they had numerous chart-topping R&B songs in the United States. Sean’s solo career began in 1995 with the album The Other Side on Atlantic Records, which peaked at #22 on the US Billboard R&B chart and #146 on the Billboard 200. 

The album produced the charting singles ‘Put Your Body Where Your Mouth Is’ (U.S. R&B #40) and ‘Same One’ (U.S. R&B #57) the same year. 

Sean married Angela Lowe and has six children —  Shareaun Woods, Keith Potts, Sean Levert Jr., Breoni Levert, Brandon Levert, and Chad Levert. In 2008, he was sentenced to 22 months in jail for failing to pay child support for three of his children, who were then ages 11, 15, and 17.

Sean fell ill while jailed at the Cuyahoga County Correctional Facility before being transferred to a state prison, suffering high blood pressure and hallucinations. Unfortunately, he died on March 30, 2008, six days after being taken to the jail. 

In May 2008, the Cuyahoga County coroner found that his death was due to complications from sarcoidosis. The official Coroner’s report also mentioned high blood pressure, heart disease, diabetes, and Xanax withdrawal. 

At the time of his death, he was 39 years old. 

Eddie Levert’s reaction to his children’s death

Like any father, Eddie was heartbroken over his children dying back to back. To deal with Gerald’s death, Eddie immersed himself in work. However, only a year and four months later, tragedy struck again when Sean died. 

Eddie adored his children, and he stated that losing them wasn’t easy. In fact, after his boys died, he wanted to blame himself because he felt he could have prevented them from experiencing certain things while they were growing up.

He told Ebony Magazine, “I wanted to really blame myself because I felt like it was some things I could’ve kept them from seeing that I did in my life, as a father.” He also confessed that he would have done anything to stop his children from dying.

He said, “Losing my boys was not an easy thing. But there’s always that thing that says, ‘God never puts anything on you that you can’t handle,’ and ‘Everything has a reason.’ And it was never in my power to stop that from happening, because I would’ve given anything and everything.”

However, he knows that he cannot dwell on losing his children forever. Instead, he holds onto their memory. 

In an interview with Cleaveland.com, he got emotional about his kids’ death and said, “I really miss them. It’s just hard. But I can’t get caught up in the fact that it wasn’t supposed to be like this because it happened.”

With his children gone, Eddie explained that he takes comfort in “God’s strength” and the music memories he shares with them. He considers his time onstage with his boys to be one of the most rewarding experiences of his life and is keeping his kids alive in his memories.
