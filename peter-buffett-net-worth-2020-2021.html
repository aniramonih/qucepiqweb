Early Life & Biography

Peter Buffett, whose full name is Peter Andrew Buffett, was born on the 4th of May 1958 in Omaha, Nebraska, the U.S. to parents, Susan Buffett and Warren Buffett. Both of his parents are popular businesspersons. His siblings are Howard Graham Buffett and Susan Alice Buffett. His brother is a famous businessman while his sister is a popular philanthropist. Coming to his education, he has attended the Omaha Central High School as well as Stanford University.

Personal Life

Peter Buffett married Mary Buffett in 1981, and Mary’s twin daughters from a previous relationship, Erica and Nicole, were four years old at the time of their wedding. He later legally adopted the twins. The couple got divorced in 1993. Three years later, he married Jennifer Buffett in June 1996, and the two have been together ever since.

Career

Peter Buffett began his career in music in the early 1980s in San Francisco, where he recorded and produced albums for local talent. He was also hired by agencies to develop commercials and logos, including channels like MTV and CNN. After being signed to Narada Productions, he released his debut album, “The Waiting,” in 1987.

He gained success by scoring the “Fire Dance” scene in the popular film, “Dances with Wolves” (1990), which went on to win the Academy Award for Best Original Score, which was composed by John Barry. Peter once again collaborated with him to compose a couple of songs for the soundtrack of the popular film, “The Scarlet Letter” (1995). After signing with Epic Records, he released the soundtrack for the 1995 documentary TV series, “500 Nations”.

In 1996, he signed with Hollywood Records and, in the following year, released the album, “Spirit Dance.” In 1999, his score for the documentary, “Wisconsin: An American Portrait,” went on to become the winner of an Emmy Award for Best Soundtrack. In the same year, he released the album “Spirit – A Journey in Dance Drums and Song,” which was successful as well.

In 2004, he released the album “Spirit –The Seventh Fire,” and two years later, he released his debut vocal album, “Gold Star.” He has his label, Beside Records, on which he released his album, “The Imaginary Kingdom” (2008). He has released upwards of 25 singles through his social networking community on Ning since 2009.

He has also collaborated with popular artists like Akon and Angélique Kidjo on songs relating to human rights. He has toured the world in support of his “Life Is What You Make It: A Concert & Conversation with Peter Buffett” performance. In May of 2010, his book, “Life Is What You Make It: Find Your Own Path to Fulfillment,” was released and was praised by several famous celebrities including Ted Turner, former President Bill Clinton, and Bill Gates.

The book sold over half a million copies worldwide and had even captured the number four position on The New York Times Best Sellers List. Peter is the co-chairman of the NoVo Foundation, which is dedicated to creating a culture of equality and partnership in a global society.
