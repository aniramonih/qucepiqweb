DDG Early Life

DDG was born Darryl Dwayne Granberry, Jr. on October 10, 1997 in Pontiac, Michigan, USA.

DDG grew up in Michigan with four brothers and sisters. His father used to work as a sound engineer in a music studio. DDG’s older brother was assassinated in 2014.

DDG Net Worth & Career in YouTube

During his college years, he considered making videos and posting them on YouTube. However, this was initially just a hobby. After a few years, DDG began to take it seriously.

DDG is a well-known rapper and businessman. DDG attended International Tech Academy, where he graduated as class valedictorian. In 2014, DDG began his YouTube career while studying at Central Michigan University.

DDG was expelled from university. However, DDG claimed to be earning more than the university’s professors. Through his YouTube videos, he was earning $30,000 per month. As a result, DDG decided to concentrate on his YouTube career.

He began on YouTube at a young age, entertaining viewers with pranks and reacting videos. His family channel provides excellent support to his career by gaining millions of subscribers. As his fame grew, he began to focus on his music and provide hits to the hip-hop community.

By investing in small businesses, he quickly became an entrepreneur. He is the best example of becoming wealthy through the use of the Internet. His early YouTube videos consist of vlogs and pranks. He flaunts his luxury cars, mansion, expensive clothing, and super expensive watches in order to inspire other kids to pursue their dreams.

DDG Music Career and History of Success

On November 23, 2017, DDG released his first single, “Givenchy.”

On November 24, 2017, WorldstarHipHop released the song’s official music video on their YouTube channel. The song has received over 20+ million YouTube views to date. On March 17, 2018, he released his first EP, “Take Me Serious.”

In June 2018, he signed a record deal with Epic Records after releasing a few singles. On June 1, 2018, he released a song titled “Arguments.” The song was a huge success. On April 15, 2020, it was even certified Gold by the RIAA. On March 22, 2019, his second EP, “Sorry 4 the Hold Up,” was released. On June 18, 2019, the official music video for the song, featuring singer Queen Naija, was released. It has over 18 million views on YouTube.

On November 1, 2019, DDG released his first studio album, “Valedictorian.” Push, the album’s first single, was released on September 20, 2019. Moonwalking in Calabasas, one of his most popular songs, will be released on July 24, 2020. The song was remixed by rappers Blueface and YG, and two versions were released.

DDG Personal Life

Kennedy Cymone, a YouTuber, was DDG’s girlfriend. They began dating in 2017.

After a year, he proposed to her in April 2018. They were also engaged, but the couple decided to split up in the summer of 2018. Prior to dating Kennedy, he was in a relationship with Emaza Dilan, a pop singer.
