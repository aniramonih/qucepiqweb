Career

Eliud came into the world of a runner during the trials for the 2002 IAAF World Cross Country Championships junior race. The event was held in Dublin, and Kipchoge finished 5th in the same. He was also a part of the junior team, which won the gold medals at the event. At the 2003 Bislett Games, he set a junior world record with a running time of 12:52.61 minutes. The album was unbroken till 2012.

He rose to popularity after he won the gold medal at the 2003 World Championships, defeated the future world record holder Kenenisa Bekele. During the 2004 Olympic Games, which was held in Athens, he won the bronze medal and was successful in making his nation proud.

In 2006, he again won a bronze medal at the World Championships, which was held in Moscow. In 2007, he won a silver medal at the World Championship, having improved his time. In the 2008 Olympic games, he won the silver medal as well have set the world record of 12:05.59. He also won the Great Yorkshire Run 10k that year. In 2018, he broke a world record at the Berlin Marathon, defeating Dennis Kimetto’s previous record of 2:02.57. In 2019, he received the Sportsman of the Year award in Kenya.
