Early Life & Biography

Natalie McIntyre was born on September 6, 1967, in Canton, Ohio, to Laura McIntyre and Otis Jones. Her father left when she was just a baby. She was raised by her mother, who was a math schoolteacher and her stepfather, who was a steelworker.

When she was seven years old, she started to learn the piano. She attended more than one school, including Canton South High School from where she graduated in the year 1985. While she was in a boarding school, she was expelled temporarily due to her behavior. Later, she went to the University of Southern California to study scriptwriting.

Personal Life

Gray married her long-time boyfriend Tracy Hinds, who was a mortgage broker in the year 1996. Together they are the parents of three beautiful children, Aanisah, Mel, and Happy. The couple divorced in 1998.

In 2005, she founded the Macy Gray Music Academy, where students can enroll and learn music.

Career

Macy Gray started her career as a cashier in Beverly Hills. Then, she met producer Joe Solo with whom she wrote and recorded many songs in Solo’s studio. Later, she got an opportunity to sing at jazz cafés.

In 1998, she signed a contract with the record label, Epic Records. The following year, she released her debut album “On How Life Is,” which was a massive hit. Her single “I Try,” which featured in the movie Picture Perfect was considered one of the biggest singles of 1999. She won the Grammy Award in the category of Best Female Pop Vocal Performance in 2001 for the same song.

The subsequent singles of the album were also highly appreciated by the audience, which helped the album become triple platinum in the U.S.

In 2001, she made her acting debut in the crime-thriller movie, Training Day. In the same year, she released her second studio album “The Id,” which peaked at number eleven on the Billboard 200 and number one on the UK Albums Chart.

Gray appeared in the “Spider-Man,” and also contributed to the song “My Nutmeg Phantasy” of the movie. Her third single “The Trouble with Being Myself” was released in 2003, which received mixed reviews.

She featured in Marcus Miller’s music album Silver Rain, and also on the soundtrack to the film Chicago. In 2007, she released her fourth studio album “Big,” which featured many popular music artists such as Natalie Cole, Justin Timberlake, Fergie, and will.i.am.

Gray composed the song “Don’t Forget Me” of the 2009 comedy film Confessions of a Shopaholic. Her next studio album, “The Sellout,” was released in 2010. The singles “Beauty in the World” and “Lately” of the album went upto number 10 on the Billboard Hot Dance Club Songs Chart.

In 2011, she signed a multi-year deal with the record label, 429 Records. Along with the record label, she released the studio album Covered in 2012. She has also featured on songs of various artists such as “Into the Deep” by Galactic, “Leave Me Lonely” by Ariana Grande, and many more.

Awards & Achievements

Macy Gray has won several awards and nominations in her illustrious career. In 2000, she won the Billboard Music Video Awards for her song “Why Didn’t You Call Me.” She has received five Grammy Award nominations, winning one.

She has also won several other awards, including Pollstar Concert Industry Awards, Independent Music Awards, MTV Video Music Awards, MTV Europe Music Awards, Brit Awards, Black Reel Awards, and many more.
