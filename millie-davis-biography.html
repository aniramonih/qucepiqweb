Who is Millie Davis?

Millie Davis is a famous Canadian film and television actress. Millie is best known for her roles as Ms. O in the PBS Kids series Odd Squad.

She has also appeared in films like ‘A Dark Truth’ and ‘Wonder’.

She won the award for Best Young Ensemble in a TV Series at the 2015 Joey Awards.

Millie Davis- Birth Age, Family

Millie Davis was born on December 6, 2006, in Toronto, Ontario, Canada. She is a Sagittarius.

Her father is Wayne Davis and her mother is Megan. Whereas, her parents run the Characters Theatre Troupe in Thornhill, Ontario.

She has a brother, Drew Davis, who is also an actor, and has appeared in Orphan Black.

Millie Davis- Professional Life, Career

Acting

She made her debut in the world of entertainment when she was a toddler of six months featuring in a Run for the Cure commercial with her father Wayne Davis.

In the year 2007, she played the voice role of Serena / Dot in the American-Canadian children’s CGI computer-animated show ‘Super Why!’.

Similarly, in 2011 she played Caitlyn McQuarrie in the TV movie ‘Befriend and Betray’.

She played one of the main voice roles of Chloe in the animated TV movie ‘The Magic Hockey Skates in 2012.

Also, Millie also featured as Saber Francis in the Damian Lee-directed and written action thriller film ‘A Dark Truth’.

The film starring Andy García, Forest Whitaker, Eva Longoria, Deborah Kara Unger, and Kim Coates premiered at the 2012 Boston Film Festival and had its theatrical release in the US on January 4, 2013.

Series

Her notable roles include that of Gemma Hendrix in the Canadian science fiction thriller television series ‘Orphan Black’.

The blockbuster hit American comedy-drama film ‘The Best Man Holiday’ written and directed by Malcolm D. Lee featured her in the role of Hope.

Drama

The American/Canadian computer-animated series ‘The Doozers’ which began airing on Hulu on April 25, 2014, features her as one of the main characters of Daisy Wheel Doozer.

Apart from that, she essayed the role of Crying Girl in the Dillon Casey-starred Canadian medical drama series ‘Remedy’ in 2014.

Also, she played the starring role of PAL in the J. J. Johnson-created Canadian CGI/live-action children’s television series ‘Annedroids’.

She landed up playing one of the most remarkable roles of her career so far, that of Ms. O in the Canadian/American children’s live-action educational television series titled ‘Odd Squad’ in 2014.

Television

Millie later reprised the role in the comedy television movie ‘Odd Squad: World Turned Odd’ which was released on January 15, 2018.

She plays the titular voice role of Esme, a monster sitter in the animated children’s television series ‘Esme & Roy’.

The series which has already aired 8 episodes premiered on August 18, 2018, and was broadcast simultaneously on Treehouse TV in Canada and on HBO in the US.
